How Battery Storage Works

Daytime Operation

During the day, solar panels generate electricity that powers your home and charges the battery system. Excess energy can be sent to the grid for credits.

Nighttime Operation

At night, when solar production stops, the battery system discharges stored energy to power your home, reducing or eliminating the need to draw from the grid.

Power Outage

During a grid outage, the battery system automatically disconnects from the grid and continues to power essential circuits in your home, providing uninterrupted electricity.

Inverter Systems

The heart of any solar energy system.

Our inverter solutions include string inverters, microinverters, hybrid inverters, and smart monitoring capabilities. We help you choose the right inverter technology for your specific needs.

Types of Inverters

Best for: Systems with consistent sunlight exposure and uniform panel orientation.

Advantages: Cost-effective, simple design, easy maintenance.

Limitations: Entire string affected by shading on a single panel.

Best for: Systems with partial shading or complex roof designs.

Advantages: Panel-level optimization, detailed monitoring, no single point of failure.

Limitations: Higher initial cost, more components on the roof.

Best for: Systems with battery storage integration.

Advantages: Manages both solar production and battery storage, simplified system design.

Limitations: More complex setup, higher cost than basic string inverters.

Best for: Balancing performance and cost.

Advantages: Panel-level optimization with central inverter, good performance in partial shading.

Limitations: Still relies on a central inverter as potential single point of failure.
